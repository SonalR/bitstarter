The above picture of an empty courtroom in the Bombay High Court taken a decade ago shows the judges podium with some of his reference books alongside and the table in front of him is laden with 2 plastic tubs full of case papers from the previous session and more alongside. This is probably after the courtroom has been cleared for this photo. Currently in 2012-13, during session the entire table is laden end to end including the table after (with the 2 lecterns) meant for seating the lawyers. All of these voluminous case papers tied with string is what the judge is supposed to read and pass judgment on after referring to all laws and judgments applicable. It is easy to see why this is a herculean task if this is one manâ€™s work as it often is. Paucity of judges aside even if we were to imagine a team of 50 people doing the same job it would be extremely difficult to read reams and reams of meandering averments or denials often poorly stated, explained and substantiated and even tougher to follow the trail of responses and correlate the evidence supplied. People evading the law use ambiguity and volume to delay the judicial process causing the current huge list of pending cases and failure of justice.
