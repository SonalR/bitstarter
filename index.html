The Problem

  1. Reduced judicial spending, results in:
    • Too few courts, judges, law colleges, jails and police to implement the law.
    • Lack of adequate digitization of court procedures
    • Lack of quality judges as sometimes good lawyers do not want to be judges as they are likely to earn more as lawyers
  2. Recruitment of the corrupt - legal, investigative and security personnel who will protect/ not prosecute the criminal recruiters are enrolled more so now that the executive i.e. the government gets a say in judicial appointments.
  3. Weakened Laws - Tampering with laws that would implicate the criminals in power

The Current Situation

Although the Indian judiciary has the following IT systems:

  1. A scheduling system (List of Business Information System (LOBIS)) that enables the Registries of Supreme Court and High Courts in eliminating manual process of Cause List generation thus any manipulation by vested interests. These databases contain details of fresh cases, disposed and pending cases. It is the backbone application of every Court.
  2. A case status information online disbursal via COURTNIC
  3. A Judgement Information System (JUDIS) consisting of complete text of all reported judgement of Supreme Court of India from 1950 to till Date.
  4. And a e-filing system in the Supreme Court and the Company Law Board in Mumbai which is the ability to submit the softcopy of your lawsuit online

It still needs further digitisation. THERE IS NO SYSTEM TO DEAL EFFECTIVELY WITH THE SUIT ITSELF. Even the softcopy of the suit has to be read just like the hard copy would, page by page.

The current format for both the civil and criminal petition/ complaint has the following parts:

  1. The introduction of the petitioners and the respondents in the civil, and the complainant and the accused in the criminal suit
  2. The articles of the Indian constitution/ penal code that apply to the petition/ complaint.
  3. How the parties to the petition/ complaint met, followed by a chronological sequence of events including the events in which the alleged crime/s happened along with their substantiation
  4. A list of prayers

The Solution

Only the above highlighted problems can be solved without the need of all/ majority policy makers concurring. Therefore the only solution to help this situation is to automate some of the tasks carried out by the lawyers and judge by digitization.

UPAMANA means to have knowledge through comparison in sanskrit. This is the name of a wizard that a lawyer uses on behalf of his clients to file either a civil petition or a criminal complaint. This wizard allows the upload of scanned supportings alongside chronological averments. The court and the judge respectively decide whether the proof uploaded is valid and whether it corroborates the averment. The respondent or accused get to respond to each averment with their own proof uploads. Again the court and judge respectively decide whether these are valid and corrobates the defence. Only the corroborated averments of both are given as a synopsis along with applicable laws and past judgments. This synopsis is presented to the judge for his verdict.

The Benefits of Digitization

  1. The court or other agencies can check whether the proof submitted is valid i.e. if they say it is a bank statement of a particular period then it is in fact that and not the drawing of a flower
    • This saves the judges time
    • Prevents people from wasting the courts time to evade justice
    • Helps the judge to narrow down the argument by paying attention to substantiated points
    • Prevents corruption as different agencies can check proofs submitted within a single suit also the same proof in a suit can be verified by different agencies and the result must be the same.
    • Allows the entity filing the suit to create a database of proofs they can refer to for the duration of the suit without having to reattach it in further replies/ affidavits.
  2. The Judge/s can check to see if the proof corroborates the averment that refers to it
    • The judge does this anyway as s the only way he can arrive at a judgement but if he can verify corroborating proofs then he can treat a denial of the same as a lie and arrive at a judgement more quickly and with much more assurance.
    • Parties in a dispute looking to evade the law by stating baseless denials and false statements will be automatically deterred from wasting every s time.
    • This will automatically lead to shorter cases and quicker and more resolute judgements
    • Less corruption as different judges/ senior councils across the state can be used to check each point also each point can be checked by multiple judges/ senior councils to get the same result.
  3. Once justice in ensured to a better extent than before many tools such a PIL (public interest litigation) can be used to effectively check government/ other mismanagement.

Filing Wizard

The wizard for the petition/ complaint can be as follows:

  1. The introductions can be automated using the UID or Aadhar number (unique identification for Indian citizens) and company registrations for corporate.
  2. A form with fields as follows: date, event description, averment, upload proof, laws/ acts/ sections or penal codes applicable (check box selection) and a selection of standard multiple choice averments under the chosen law that the averment described is the same as.
  3. A List of Prayers

Judicial Processing

  1. The system must make a distinction must be made between substantiated and non substantiated averments.
  2. It is the courts job to validate the substantiations either directly or through agencies. The system must make a distinction between valid and invalid substantiations.
  3. Invalid proofs should be fined Rs.2,000/- each.
  4. The judge then must decide whether the valid substantiation submitted corroborates the averment
  5. The system must make a distinction between corroborated and non corroborated averments

The Response

The wizard for the response to the petition/ complaint can be as follows:

  1. Alongside the petition/ complaint averment rows the respondent/ accused either accepts of denies the event description and/ or averment.
  2. If he denies then he is allowed to submit his own version of events/ rebuttal with supporting evidences.
  3. The same court and judicial process follows for the denial and fresh description.
  4. Denial of a corroborated averment without their submitting proof is recognized as a lie and should attract a fine of Rs. 8,000/-

UPAMANA

All of the above information is arranged tabularly for the most effective comparison.

Finally based on the judge deciding whether the averments for or against is corroborated the system presents only the corroborated averments chronologically to the judge for his consideration. The laws and past judgments applicable to each is also listed.
